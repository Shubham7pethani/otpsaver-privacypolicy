Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 15, 2026

Orbit Labs Tech ("we", "our", or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting it. This Privacy Policy explains our practices regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information when you use the OTP Saver - Auto OTP Manager mobile application (the "App").

Our core principle: Your data never leaves your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store your personal data on any external servers.

1. Data We Access and Why

To provide its core functionality of automatically detecting, saving, and managing One-Time Passwords (OTPs), the App requires specific device permissions:

  • SMS Permissions (RECEIVE_SMS, READ_SMS): The App uses these permissions strictly to listen for incoming SMS messages containing OTPs. The App processes these messages entirely locally on your device to extract the password and the sender's name.
  • Notification Permissions (POST_NOTIFICATIONS): Used to alert you instantly when a new OTP originates from an SMS message, allowing you to copy it directly from your notification panel.

2. Local Storage Only

All extracted OTPs and associated metadata (such as sender name and timestamp) are stored securely in a local database entirely on your device. We do not use cloud synchronization, analytics trackers, or external databases. If you uninstall the app or clear its data, all saved OTPs are permanently deleted.

3. No Data Sharing

Because the App operates 100% offline and does not connect to the internet, it is technically impossible for us to share, sell, or transmit your SMS data, OTPs, or personal information to any third party.

4. Children's Privacy

Our App does not address anyone under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children. Because we collect no data at all, no children's data is ever transmitted from the device.

5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date at the top.
